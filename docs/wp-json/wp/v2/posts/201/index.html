The Homecoming!<\/h2>\n

Please note the new (old) venue. I’m pleased to announce that the Stafford Ale House is the new venue (ex Pie and Ale House). Same place – with much the same staff I believe.<\/p>\n

This is a result as the facilities are much more suitable for our needs – certainly for workshop style meetings such as this months.<\/p>\n

CRC Cards<\/h2>\n

This month we have a guest speaker, Sam Wessel<\/a>. Sam is a founder member of our group, an ex Codeweaver<\/a> and now is working in Nottingham for Esendex Ltd<\/a>. He has presented at conferences such as the Scottish Ruby Conference<\/a> , Agile North<\/a> and is also actively involved with GeekUp Nottingham<\/a>.<\/p>\n

Sam will be leading a workshop style session providing a practical introduction into CRC cards.<\/p>\n

Wikipedia states<\/a>:<\/p>\n

Class Responsibility Collaboration (CRC) cards are a brainstorming tool used in the design of object-oriented software. They were proposed by Ward Cunningham and Kent Beck. They are typically used when first determining which classes are needed and how they will interact.<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

My perspective is that the CRC technique can be incredibly useful on a number of counts:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Assists and reveals an OO design<\/li>\n
  • Provides the team with a common understanding of the design and behaviour of a system<\/li>\n
  • Can be useful as an OO teaching tool<\/li>\n
  • Can be particularly useful for Visual Thinkers<\/a><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    All you need is a pen – there will be no coding. However I bet a number of us will want to code the designs we come up with!<\/p>\n

    Hope to see you there,
    \nNeil Kidd<\/p>\n
